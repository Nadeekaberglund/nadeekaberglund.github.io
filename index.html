Academic Research

Master's Thesis

Navigating the Competitive Landscape: The Role of Outsourcing in Organizational Success

This research examined how strategic outsourcing influences organizational competitiveness within service supply chains in Sweden. Using qualitative research methods and grounded theory, the study explored the opportunities, challenges, and critical factors that organizations should consider when outsourcing both core and non-core business activities.

The research resulted in a comprehensive framework that helps organizations balance the benefits of outsourcing while addressing challenges such as quality issues, additional costs, and delivery risks, supporting more effective strategic decision-making.

Bachelor's Thesis

Factors Influencing the Application of Consumers' Prior Ecolabel Knowledge in Their Green Consumption

This research explored the factors that influence how consumers apply their knowledge of ecolabels when making environmentally conscious purchasing decisions in Sweden. Using qualitative research methods and semi-structured interviews, the study examined the motivations and barriers that shape sustainable consumer behaviour.

The findings revealed that environmental awareness, ecolabel knowledge, health considerations, and label credibility encourage sustainable purchasing decisions, while price sensitivity, limited product availability, and lack of knowledge can discourage green consumption. The research provides valuable insights for businesses, marketers, and policymakers seeking to promote sustainable consumer behaviour.

Professional Projects

The Role of Digital and Above-The-Line (ATL) Activities in Establishing a Robust Bond Between Brands and Consumers

Project Type: Professional Marketing Analysis

This report examines how organizations can strengthen relationships between their brands and consumers by integrating traditional advertising (Above-the-Line marketing) with digital marketing strategies. It explores how multiple communication channels work together to increase brand awareness, encourage consumer engagement, and build long-term customer loyalty.

Drawing on established theories of consumer behaviour and marketing, the report explains how consumers develop relationships with brands through awareness, experience, emotional connection, identity, and community engagement. It also highlights the importance of combining traditional media with digital platforms to create consistent and meaningful customer experiences.

The analysis identifies storytelling, clear brand positioning, interactive digital content, user-generated content, and online reviews as key factors in strengthening brand recognition, consumer trust, and emotional attachment. These elements contribute to stronger customer engagement and influence purchasing decisions.

Based on the theoretical analysis, the report recommends adopting an integrated marketing communication strategy that combines traditional advertising with digital media to deliver consistent brand messages across multiple channels. The report concludes that organizations can strengthen brand loyalty, improve customer engagement, and support sustainable business growth through customer-centred marketing strategies.

The Power of Storytelling in Marketing: How Brands Create Emotional Connections

Storytelling has become one of the most powerful tools in modern marketing because humans naturally understand and remember information through stories. Unlike traditional communication that focuses only on facts and product features, storytelling creates emotional connections by linking brands with experiences, values, and memorable moments.

In today's competitive business environment, companies use storytelling to strengthen brand awareness, increase customer engagement, and build long-term relationships with consumers. Through successful storytelling campaigns, brands can communicate their messages in a more meaningful way and create experiences that customers remember.

This article explores the role of storytelling in marketing, its psychological impact on consumer behaviour, and how companies such as Sunsilk and Lifebuoy have successfully used storytelling strategies to connect with their target audiences.

According to Schollers (Woodside, 2010), human memory operates on a story-based structure, where information is organized, stored, and retrieved as narratives. This view emphasizes that much of the data within these stories is directly tied to experiences, emotions, or outcomes linked to a specific context. Scholars also suggest that certain brands can trigger distinct memories in consumers, evoking feelings of satisfaction or pleasure.

Storytelling is a powerful marketing tool that fosters an emotional connection between companies and customers, effectively communicating brand messages and boosting customer awareness and engagement.

From a psychological standpoint, presenting information as a story is more effective than merely offering facts, as stories are more memorable and resonate deeply with the human brain. In some countries, teachers use storytelling to convey subject matter, helping students memorize and understand content better.

When a story is told, the listener’s brain mirrors the patterns of the storyteller’s brain in a phenomenon called ”neural coupling” or ”mirroring.” Research by Greg J. Stephens, Lauren J. Silbert, and Uri Hasson shows that this process involves multiple brain areas, leading to a shared understanding. Additionally, the motor, sensory, and frontal cortices work together during storytelling, creating anticipation while releasing dopamine, the brain’s “feel-good” chemical, enhancing the experience (https://neuroleadership.com/).

Consequently, marketing communicators and marketers recognize storytelling as an effective strategy. Companies can incorporate storytelling into Above-the-Line (ATL) campaigns and digital media efforts to create engaging narratives. Presenting brand messages as stories helps capture and retain consumer attention. Tailoring these narratives to specific target groups increases their impact, making storytelling vital for building brand resonance.

Furthermore, storytelling emphasizes the role of word-of-mouth (WOM) communication in establishing trust and credibility. Woodside et al. (2008) identify WOM as a powerful way to spread messages. A Nielsen survey supports this, revealing that while only 33% of consumers trust online ads, 90% trust recommendations from friends, and 70% trust electronic WOM (eWOM) (Fill, 2016). Companies can foster trust by leveraging eWOM through digital channels like social media campaigns, strengthening connections and loyalty.
